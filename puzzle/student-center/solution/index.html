This puzzle is the supermeta for the Students round, which consists of 54 student puzzles that are unlocked by finding them around campus. These puzzles feed into dorm metas, one for each of the 10 undergraduate dorms. There is additionally a metameta for the 7 dorms on Dorm Row (in particular, only the Dorm Row, East Campus, Random Hall, and Simmons Hall metas have answers that describe how to fix their respective problems).

Each of the meta answers solves a specific problem associated with its building(s) which is associated with one of air, fire, earth, or water (this is also depicted in the art for that submeta/subround). To solve their problem, they get help from one of the other three sub-rounds. Specifically:

  • Air: The air conditioning in the Dorm Row buildings is currently broken so the rooms are too cold. The solution is to CALL EC’S DRAGONS to warm things up.
  • Fire: East Campus’s fire pit is currently running low on fuel. Use Random’s RANDOM LUMBER GENERATOR to get some more.
  • Earth: Random is currently full of dirt. They should DISTRIBUTE SPONGES from Simmons to clean things up.
  • Water: Simmons is flooded! Use A ROW BLOW from Dorm Row to remove the excess water.

Each of the students are named, and when you solve their corresponding puzzle, you learn which clubs they belong to. This information has not been used anywhere else so far.

The flavortext mentions a student sitting at a “strangely shaped table,” and the problems / solutions to the four dorm metas involved the four classical elements. These two facts should make us think of using the periodic table of elements to proceed.

It turns out that we can associate each student with a particular element with atomic number between 1 and 54 inclusive. The clubs that a student belongs to represent various properties of the elements. For example, the Honor Society consists of all the noble gases. As further confirmation, the length of each student’s name matches the length of their corresponding element.

Using the clubs and the names, it becomes a logic puzzle of sorts to identify which student corresponds to which element. For example, the only member of the Singles Club who isn’t in the Start-a-Club Club is Nathaniel, which identifies him as Potassium, as it’s the only element with atomic number less than 54 whose single letter abbreviation (K) is not also its first letter. Note that Nathaniel and Potassium are both nine letters.
