This puzzle is about Increasingly Verbose memes, a format in which increasingly wordy restatements of a simple phrase are paired with increasingly bad (or JPEG-compressed) drawings of something related to that phrase. This is clued by the fact that the drawings in the puzzle come in pairs, with the bottom image in each pair drawn worse than the top one, and also by the fact that the title of the puzzle, Successively More Abundant in Verbiage, is itself a verbosification of "increasingly verbose."

Each pair of drawings is associated with a literary classic, either from its plot-related motifs or from its book cover. For example, the two first drawings depict guillotines, which clues A Tale of Two Cities by Charles Dickens, and the last two drawings depict balls of grey twine, which clues To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee. Each image also contains a distinct roman numeral from 1 to 32 inclusive. The images are sorted alphabetically by book title.

However, the verbose paragraphs do not come in pairs, and it is up to the solver to find two verbose paragraphs for each literary classic that state a famous quote from that classic. In keeping with the flavor of the meme, the more verbose paragraph is always associated with the lower, worse-drawn image, and hence a larger roman numeral. But as an added twist, each paragraph is not a direct verbosification of the original quote, but rather of modified quote in which one of the words has a letter replaced. For example, this paragraph:

It was simultaneously the superlative among coin currencies displaying benign properties, as well the superlative among coin currencies displaying malignant properties.

Is a lengthier version of:

It was the best of dimes, it was the worst of dimes.

Which is a modification of the first sentence from A Tale of Two Cities. The full set of associations between verbose paragraphs and literary quotes is as follows.

I have a soft spot for puzzles about memes. Not only are memes hilarious, they also convey implicit information through imagery and position in a neatly-packed way, which is often true of puzzles as well. This puzzle was somewhat inspired by another meme puzzle: Now Get Ready For... from Galactic Puzzle Hunt 2018. Drawings images for this puzzle was hard because given my artistic skills, both the top image and the bottom image end up looking bad, yet I need the top image to look relatively less bad. On the other hand, writing verbose paragraphs is tremendously fun, and I had to force myself to stay under 300 characters for the shorter paragraphs, and 500 characters for the longer paragraphs.

For the final extraction, I knew I wanted to clue a verbosification of a quote from Romeo and Juliet, and ended up choosing HEAVENLY BODY INTERSECTED PARAMOURS because STAR CROSSED LOVERS is more than just a memorable quote, it is an epithet for the protagonists themselves. That said, I did consider alternate quotes such as I MASTICATE MY FIRST DIGIT AT THEE, or O FELICITOUS POIGNARD THINE SCABBARD IS THE FOLLOWING.
